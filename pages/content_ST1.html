Intro / Hook Line

Beneath the waves, an entire world thrives—colorful, busy, and alive. These are coral reefs, the underwater cities of our planet.

🐠 What are Coral Reefs?

  • Coral reefs are massive underwater structures made from the skeletons of tiny sea animals called coral polyps.
  • They cover less than 1% of the ocean floor but support about 25% of all marine life.
  • Reefs are like apartment complexes for ocean life—providing food, shelter, and breeding grounds.

🌍 Why Are They Called Underwater Cities?

  • Just like cities on land, coral reefs have complex networks where different organisms play different roles.
  • They have “residents” (fish, crustaceans, mollusks), “builders” (corals), “cleaners” (shrimp, fish), and even “police” (predators that keep the balance).
  • Reefs have traffic too—schools of fish zooming around like rush hour.

🌱 Why Are Coral Reefs Important?

  • They protect coastlines from storms and erosion.
  • Provide food and income for over 500 million people.
  • Are crucial for medicine—many treatments are derived from reef organisms.
  • They're biodiversity hotspots—more diverse than rainforests.

⚠️ Threats to Coral Reefs

  • Climate change is causing coral bleaching—reefs lose their color and life.
  • Pollution, overfishing, and irresponsible tourism are also damaging reefs.
  • If we don’t act, most reefs could vanish in our lifetime.

💙 How Can We Help?

  • Reduce plastic and chemical waste.
  • Support reef-friendly products and tourism.
  • Raise awareness—every small action matters.
